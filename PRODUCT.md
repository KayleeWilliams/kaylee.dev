# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Anyone who lands on kaylee.dev wanting to understand who Kaylee Williams is: peer engineers evaluating her craft or considering c15t, recruiters and hiring managers sizing up scope and range, founders and potential collaborators in the privacy/consent space, and increasingly AI agents and answer engines summarizing "who is Kaylee Williams." The site is not optimized for a single funnel; it is a strong, memorable personal brand that reads well to all of them. Visitors arrive curious and skim fast, so the first screen has to land identity, credibility, and personality at a glance.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for a founding engineer at Inth (YC P26) and co-author of c15t, the open-source consent layer. It exists to establish identity and credibility instantly, showcase the work that backs it up (c15t and the broader Inth project surface, real OSS activity, speaking), and do all of it with enough taste and personality to be memorable. Success: a visitor leaves able to say what Kaylee builds, see proof she is good at it, and remember the site as distinctly hers.

## Brand Personality

Precise, playful, design-minded. Technically sharp and confident, but with earned whimsy and visible taste. The voice is clean and self-assured (lets the work and the metrics carry weight) with one or two genuine winks, never a stream of them. Confident, not loud. Warm, not corporate.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-slop portfolio** — identical card grids, the hero-metric template, the interchangeable "modern dev portfolio." No point of view.
- **A clone of burnedchris.com** — her founder/boss's bold editorial style. Kaylee's site must be unmistakably her own taste, not a borrowed silhouette.
- **Try-hard cringe** — emoji buzzword soup, scrolling tickers, "TypeScript Maximalist 💜" labels that announce personality instead of demonstrating it.
- **Corporate / sterile** — over-polished agency-template feel with no warmth or personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't announce.** Personality comes through specific, earned details (the cat reveal, the c15t footer joke, a real `console.log` in devtools), never through labels that declare "I'm quirky."
2. **Practice what you preach.** She builds privacy/consent infrastructure; the site itself runs privacy-first, needs no cookie banner, and is fast and well-crafted. The site is a proof of the work.
3. **Own her taste, not his.** Distinct from the founder/editorial register of her boss. Her lane is precise-but-playful with purple and rounded pills as signature, not bold-serif-founder.
4. **Let the work carry the weight.** Real evidence (stars, downloads, shipped products, live PR activity, talks) over adjectives. Confidence is earned from proof, not asserted.
5. **Earned whimsy only.** One or two delightful moments executed excellently beat scattershot quirk. Every playful element must reward attention and be specific to Kaylee.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Maintain sufficient contrast in both light and dark themes (the purple accent must stay legible on both). Respect `prefers-color-scheme` and `prefers-reduced-motion` (already honored: marquee/flip/grid animations gate on reduced-motion). Keyboard-navigable links and controls, meaningful `alt` text and `aria-label`s on icon-only controls, visible focus states.
